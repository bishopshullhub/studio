Bishops Hull Hub

Booking Process Flow

How a hire enquiry moves from first contact to confirmed event — for reference by the Board of Trustees

👤 Customer Journey — Online Booking Form
1
🌐

Customer visits the website

They click "Hire the Hub" in the menu and arrive at the booking page. They can see pricing at a glance before starting.

  • Hourly hire: £18 / hour
  • Full day (8+ hours): £140 flat rate
2
📅

Step 1 — Checks live availability

A live calendar is displayed showing all existing bookings. The customer can browse to their preferred date to see if it is free before filling in any details.

3
📋

Step 2 — Reads & acknowledges policies

The customer must tick a box confirming they have read the key rules before proceeding:

  • Bouncy castle requirements
  • No fireworks permitted (proximity to homes)
4
👤

Step 3 — Enters contact details

Full name, email address, phone number, home address & postcode, and their preferred contact method (email or phone).

5
🎉

Step 4 — Enters event details

They fill in what they need for the actual event:

  • Type of event (e.g. Birthday Party, Community Meeting)
  • Date required — must be at least 14 days from today
  • Start and end time (in 15-minute slots, earliest start 08:00)
  • Estimated attendance — maximum 110 people

As they type, the website automatically checks the live calendar for clashes and calculates the estimated hire cost.

🔄

System auto-checks: is the slot free?

Instantly queries the live calendar. If there is already a booking at that time, a red warning is shown — the customer is advised to pick a different time.

6
✏️

Step 5 — Additional requirements & agreement

Any special requests (e.g. kitchen access, bar service). The customer must tick to agree to the Standard Conditions of Hire before they can submit.

7
📤

Customer clicks "Submit Enquiry"

The form is sent. An Enquiry Reference Number is automatically generated (e.g. a3f82b1c).

⚙️ Automatic System Actions (happens instantly, behind the scenes)

Two things happen at the same time, automatically:

🗄️
Saved to database
All enquiry details are stored securely, marked as "Pending" status.
📧
Two emails sent
(1) Confirmation email to the customer.
(2) Notification email to our bookings secretary.
✅

Customer sees confirmation screen

A "What happens next?" page is shown with clear steps, their enquiry summary, and a unique reference number they can keep for their records.

🏢 Bookings Secretary (Admin)
📬

Secretary receives notification email

An email arrives with all the enquiry details. The secretary logs into the secure admin portal on the website to see all enquiries in a dashboard (similar to a Kanban board).

👀

Secretary reviews the enquiry

Status is moved from "Pending" → "Reviewed". The secretary checks the calendar, verifies the date is genuinely available, and assesses the request.

Target: respond to all enquiries within 3 working days.

Decision Point
Can we accommodate this booking?
✗ No / Unable to help
❌

Enquiry rejected

Status set to "Rejected". Customer is contacted to explain and, where possible, suggest an alternative date.

✓ Yes — proceed
📞

Secretary contacts customer

By the customer's preferred method (email or phone). Availability & event details are confirmed verbally or in writing.

💳 Payment & Booking Confirmation
🧾

Hire charge invoice sent to customer

The customer is invoiced for the full hire charge to secure the date. Payment is due within 14 days of the invoice date, or before the event — whichever is sooner.

  • Hourly hire: £18 / hour
  • Full day (8+ hours): £140 flat rate
💰

Customer pays hire charge

Once the hire charge is received the booking is officially secured.

🗓️

Booking confirmed on admin portal & calendar

Status is moved to "Confirmed". The date is added to the live calendar — it will now show as unavailable for any future enquiries.

🔔

Refundable deposit requested — 2 weeks before the event

Two weeks before the event date, the customer is asked to pay a refundable security deposit:

  • Events finishing before 8pm: £50 deposit
  • Events finishing after 8pm: £100 deposit

This deposit is held against any damage or cleaning costs, and returned within 7 days after the event if all is well.

💳

Customer pays refundable deposit

Deposit received and held. The event can now proceed as planned.

🎊 The Event & After
🎉

Event takes place

Hirer is responsible for the venue during their booked time (including set-up and clear-up time). They must leave the hall clean and tidy before their hire period ends.

  • Maximum 110 people in the building
  • No fireworks, no illegal substances
  • No smoking inside or in the paved area
🔍

Post-event venue inspection

The Hub team inspects the premises after the event.

Decision Point
Is the venue left in a satisfactory condition?
✗ Damage / Cleaning needed
🧹

Costs deducted from deposit

Cleaning: £15/hr (before 8pm) or £20/hr (after 8pm) — minimum 1 hour. Photo evidence taken. Remainder of deposit returned.

✓ All good
💚

Full deposit returned

Refunded to the hirer within 7 days of the event.

✓ Booking Complete
